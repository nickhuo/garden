## Part 2 — Research Radar (HuggingFace Papers, English)

### TL;DR — Today's Research Wind
Three currents dominate today's HF Daily: (1) **self-evolving skill-based agents** — three independent papers (Skill1, SkillOS, Auto Research with Specialist Agents) all converge on "policy that learns to curate its own skill library from outcome-only signals"; (2) **GRPO-era post-training surgery** — four papers (LoPE, A²TGPO, Balanced Aggregation, ScaleLogic) target specific failure modes of vanilla GRPO (zero-advantage, turn-level credit, aggregation bias, length scaling); (3) **continuous diffusion creeping into language modeling** — Cola DLM and CDM extend diffusion machinery into territory long owned by autoregressive transformers, with matched-FLOP scaling claims. Worth flagging that SimpleAudit (benchmarkless safety scoring) and KernelBench-X (GPU kernel generation eval) are the two most interview-quotable evals of the day.

### Agent frameworks & self-evolution

#### 🟢 [2605.05724] Auto Research with Specialist Agents
- **Problem:** Closed-loop research agents drift without lineage; one-shot proposals waste evaluator outcomes.
- **Method:** Specialist agents partition recipe surfaces; lineage feedback turns crashes/budget/score into program-level edits.
- **Key result:** −0.81% Parameter Golf bpb; +38.7% NanoChat-D12 CORE; −4.59% CIFAR-10 Airbench96 wallclock; humans untouched during 1,797 trials.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.05724) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.05724) · [GitHub](https://github.com/cxcscmu/Auto-Research-Recipes) ⭐1

#### 🟢 [2605.06130] Skill1: Unified Evolution of Skill-Augmented Agents via RL
- **Problem:** Skill selection / utilization / distillation are usually optimized in isolation, producing conflicting evolution.
- **Method:** Single policy generates query, re-ranks library candidates, solves, distills new skill — all from one task-outcome signal.
- **Key result:** Outperforms prior skill and RL baselines on ALFWorld and WebShop (no headline numbers in abstract).
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.06130) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.06130)

#### 🟢 [2605.06614] SkillOS: Learning Skill Curation for Self-Evolving Agents
- **Problem:** Skill curation under delayed feedback is the bottleneck of self-evolving agents; manual or heuristic curation breaks down on long horizons.
- **Method:** Frozen executor + trainable curator updating an external SkillRepo; composite rewards over grouped task streams.
- **Key result:** Outperforms memory-free and memory-based baselines on multi-turn agentic + single-turn reasoning tasks (no quantitative result reported in abstract).
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.06614) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.06614)

#### 🟡 [2605.06651] AI Co-Mathematician: Accelerating Mathematicians with Agentic AI
- **Problem:** Mathematicians need stateful, exploratory AI that tracks failed hypotheses, not chat-style assistants.
- **Method:** Asynchronous workspace with intent refinement, lineage-aware artifacts, native math output formats.
- **Key result:** 48% on FrontierMath Tier 4 — new high among evaluated AI systems.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.06651) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.06651)

### Agent reliability, evaluation & safety

#### 🟢 [2605.04956] KernelBench-X: Comprehensive Benchmark for LLM-Generated GPU Kernels
- **Problem:** No category-aware benchmark for Triton kernel generation across correctness × efficiency × hardware.
- **Method:** 176 tasks across 15 categories evaluated on 5 representative methods, with per-category variance decomposition.
- **Key result:** Category explains 9.4% vs method 3.3% deviance; 72% of Fusion tasks fail across all methods; 46.6% of "correct" kernels are slower than PyTorch eager.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.04956) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.04956) · [GitHub](https://github.com/BonnieW05/KernelBenchX) ⭐14

#### 🟡 [2605.06652] When No Benchmark Exists: Validating Comparative LLM Safety Scoring
- **Problem:** Pre-deployment safety comparison without ground-truth labels has no validity contract.
- **Method:** Instrumental-validity chain (safe-vs-abliterated contrast, target-driven variance, rerun stability) implemented in SimpleAudit.
- **Key result:** AUROC 0.89–1.00 separating safe vs abliterated; target identity η²≈0.52 of variance; severity stable by 10 reruns.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.06652) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.06652) · [GitHub](https://github.com/kelkalot/simpleaudit) ⭐14

### Post-training (RL, distillation, merging, calibration)

#### 🟢 [2605.05566] Nonsense Helps: Prompt Space Perturbation Broadens Reasoning Exploration (LoPE)
- **Problem:** GRPO's zero-advantage problem on hard queries: all rollouts fail, training signal collapses.
- **Method:** LoPE prepends Lorem-Ipsum-style task-irrelevant tokens to perturb output distribution before resampling.
- **Key result:** Outperforms vanilla resampling across 1.7B / 4B / 7B models; other low-perplexity Latin sequences also work.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.05566) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.05566)

#### 🟢 [2605.06200] A²TGPO: Agentic Turn-Group Policy Optimization
- **Problem:** Multi-turn RL credit assignment is sparse; existing IG-based methods distort across heterogeneous turn contexts.
- **Method:** Turn-group normalization + variance-rescaled discounted accumulation + adaptive turn-level clipping on Information Gain.
- **Key result:** No quantitative result reported in abstract.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.06200) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.06200) · [GitHub](https://github.com/CuSO4-Chen/A-TGPO) ⭐1

#### 🟢 [2605.04077] Balanced Aggregation: Fixing GRPO Aggregation Bias
- **Problem:** GRPO's choice of token vs sequence aggregation introduces opposite optimization biases (sign-length coupling vs implicit length downweighting).
- **Method:** Balanced Aggregation — token-level means computed separately on positive/negative subsets, recombined with sequence-count weights.
- **Key result:** Improves stability and final accuracy across 6 reasoning + coding benchmarks on Qwen2.5-Math-7B / Qwen3-1.7B (no headline delta in abstract).
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.04077) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.04077)

#### 🟢 [2605.06638] Can RL Teach Long-Horizon Reasoning to LLMs? Expressiveness Is Key
- **Problem:** No controlled environment to study how RL training compute scales with reasoning depth × logical expressiveness.
- **Method:** ScaleLogic synthetic framework with independent depth and expressiveness axes; multiple RL methods + curricula.
- **Key result:** Compute T ∝ D^γ (R²>0.99); γ rises from 1.04 (implication-only) to 2.60 (full first-order); +10.66 points on math/general reasoning under expressive training.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.06638) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.06638)

#### 🟡 [2605.06507] MARBLE: Multi-Aspect Reward Balance for Diffusion RL
- **Problem:** Weighted-sum aggregation of multiple rewards in diffusion RL dilutes specialist supervision.
- **Method:** Per-reward independent advantage estimators harmonized via a quadratic program; amortized for near single-reward cost.
- **Key result:** SD3.5-Medium with 5 rewards: improves all five simultaneously; flips worst-aligned gradient cosine from negative→positive in 80% of mini-batches; 0.97× baseline training speed.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.06507) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.06507) · [GitHub](https://github.com/aim-uofa/MARBLE) ⭐5

#### 🟡 [2605.06376] Continuous-Time Distribution Matching for Few-Step Diffusion Distillation
- **Problem:** Discrete-anchor DMD generates artifacts and over-smoothed outputs without auxiliary GAN/reward modules.
- **Method:** CDM enforces distribution matching at random continuous timesteps + off-trajectory matching via student velocity field.
- **Key result:** Competitive few-step fidelity on SD3-Medium and Longcat-Image without auxiliary objectives (no quantitative result in abstract).
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.06376) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.06376) · [GitHub](https://github.com/byliutao/cdm) ⭐1

### Inference optimization & compiler

#### 🟡 [2605.06665] UniPool: A Globally Shared Expert Pool for Mixture-of-Experts
- **Problem:** Per-layer MoE expert ownership couples depth scaling to linear parameter growth — and per-layer routers add little.
- **Method:** Single globally-shared expert pool accessed via independent per-layer routers, with NormRouter and pool-level auxiliary loss.
- **Key result:** Across 5 LLaMA scales (182M–978M, 30B Pile tokens) UniPool reduces validation loss by up to 0.0386 vs vanilla MoE; 41.6%–66.7% of expert-parameter budget matches/beats baseline.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.06665) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.06665) · [GitHub](https://github.com/Centaurus-Alpha/UniPool) ⭐3

#### ⚪ [2605.06548] Continuous Latent Diffusion Language Model (Cola DLM)
- **Problem:** Non-AR alternatives to AR LMs fail to jointly hit efficiency, scalable representation, and global semantic modeling.
- **Method:** Hierarchical latent diffusion: Text VAE → block-causal DiT prior in continuous latent → conditional decoding.
- **Key result:** Strong scaling behavior up to ~2B params / ~2000 EFLOPs vs matched AR + LLaDA baselines (no single-number headline).
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.06548) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.06548)

### Retrieval / RAG / memory

#### 🟢 [2605.06416] MiA-Signature: Approximating Global Activation for Long-Context
- **Problem:** Long-context inference cannot afford full activation transport; conscious-cognition analog suggests a compressed substitute.
- **Method:** Submodular selection of high-level concepts covering activated context; iterative refinement via working memory; injected as conditioning signal.
- **Key result:** Consistent gains across multiple long-context tasks when integrated into RAG and agentic systems (no metric in abstract).
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.06416) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.06416)

#### 🟡 [2605.04523] RaguTeam at SemEval-2026 Task 8: Judge-Orchestrated LLM Ensemble
- **Problem:** Faithful multi-turn RAG generation needs more than single-model decoding.
- **Method:** Heterogeneous ensemble of 7 LLMs × 2 prompting variants; GPT-4o-mini judge picks best per instance; introduces Meno-Lite-0.1 (7B domain-adapted).
- **Key result:** 1st of 26 teams; conditioned harmonic mean 0.7827 vs strongest baseline gpt-oss-120b at 0.6390.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.04523) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.04523) · [GitHub](https://github.com/RaguTeam/ragu_mtrag_semeval) ⭐0

### Multimodal, world models, generation

#### 🟡 [2605.06222] When to Trust Imagination: Adaptive WAM Execution
- **Problem:** World Action Models execute fixed-length action chunks regardless of physical-vs-imagined drift.
- **Method:** FFDC verifier reasons over predicted future actions + visual dynamics + real obs + language to gate chunk length; Mixture-of-Horizon Training.
- **Key result:** RoboTwin: −69.10% WAM forward passes, −34.02% execution time, +2.54% success vs short-chunk baseline; +35% real-world success.

#### 🟡 [2605.04962] TabEmbed: Generalist Embeddings for Tabular Understanding
- **Problem:** No retrieval-compatible foundation embedding for tabular data; text embeddings miss structure & numerical semantics.
- **Method:** Reformulate tabular tasks as semantic matching; large-scale contrastive learning with positive-aware hard-negative mining; new TabBench.
- **Key result:** Outperforms SOTA text embedding models on TabBench (no quantitative headline).
