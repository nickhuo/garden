## Part 2 — Research Radar (HuggingFace Papers, English)

### TL;DR — Today's Research Wind

Today's drop is unusually agent-heavy on the post-training and reliability axis: at least three papers attack agentic RL (LPO, AEM, HyperEyes) and two more attack the "what happens after the agent ships" problem (PrefixGuard for online failure-warning, DTap for red-teaming). The other dominant thread is long-context inference cost — four separate papers (MISA, SPEED, UniPrefill, SpecBlock) all reduce TTFT / KV-cache / drafter cost from different angles. Notably absent: foundation-model bench-chasing.

### Agent frameworks & self-evolution

#### 🟢 [2605.08083] AutoTTS — LLMs Improving LLMs for Test-Time Scaling
- **Problem:** Test-time scaling strategies are hand-crafted; the controller design space is barely explored.
- **Method:** Environment-driven framework that searches width-depth TTS controllers as synthesis over pre-collected reasoning trajectories with cheap probe-based feedback.
- **Key result:** Discovers strategies beating manual TTS on math benchmarks; total discovery cost $39.9 / 160 min, generalizes to held-out scales.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.08083) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.08083) · [GitHub](https://github.com/zhengkid/AutoTTS) ⭐16

#### 🟢 [2605.06716] Survey — Evolution of LLM Agent Memory Mechanisms
- **Problem:** Agent-memory research is fragmented between OS engineering and cognitive-science framings.
- **Method:** Three-stage evolutionary framework (Storage → Reflection → Experience), with proactive exploration and cross-trajectory abstraction as Experience-stage primitives.
- **Key result:** No quantitative result reported — survey paper offering design principles.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.06716) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.06716) · [GitHub](https://github.com/FeishuLuo/Evolving-LLM-Agent-Memory-Survey) ⭐9

#### 🟡 [2605.06924] A²RD — Agentic Autoregressive Diffusion for Long Video
- **Problem:** Long video synthesis suffers semantic drift and narrative collapse over long horizons.
- **Method:** Retrieve–Synthesize–Refine–Update loop with multimodal video memory, adaptive segment generation, hierarchical test-time self-improvement.
- **Key result:** Up to 30% consistency / 20% narrative-coherence improvement over SOTA on 1–10 min videos (LVBench-C).
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.06924) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.06924)

### Agent reliability, evaluation & safety

#### 🟢 [2605.04808] DTap — Red-Teaming Platform for AI Agents
- **Problem:** No realistic, controllable environments for large-scale agent risk assessment exist.
- **Method:** 14-domain, 50-environment platform (Workspace, PayPal, Slack simulacra) + DTap-Red autonomous red-teaming agent exploring prompt/tool/skill/env injections.
- **Key result:** No headline metric; releases DTap-Bench with verifiable judges and reveals "systematic vulnerability patterns" across popular agents.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.04808) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.04808) · [GitHub](https://github.com/AI-secure/DecodingTrust-Agent) ⭐16

#### 🟢 [2605.06455] PrefixGuard — Agent Traces to Online Failure Monitors
- **Problem:** Final-outcome checks for tool-using agents arrive too late; hand-authored event schemas are brittle.
- **Method:** Offline StepView typed-step induction + supervised prefix-risk scorer trained from terminal outcomes; derives observability ceiling.
- **Key result:** 0.900 / 0.710 / 0.533 / 0.557 AUPRC on WebArena / τ²-Bench / SkillsBench / TerminalBench; +0.137 AUPRC over raw-text controls.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.06455) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.06455) · [GitHub](https://github.com/shinmohuang/PrefixGuard) ⭐1

#### 🟡 [2605.07447] SAEgis — Sparse Autoencoders as Firewalls in VLMs
- **Problem:** VLM agents are highly vulnerable to adversarial image attacks; existing defenses generalize poorly.
- **Method:** Insert SAE module into pretrained VLM, train with standard reconstruction; sparse latent features double as attack-signal classifier.
- **Key result:** Strong in-domain, cross-domain, and cross-attack detection; "particularly large" cross-domain gains (no scalar reported).
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.07447) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.07447) · [GitHub](https://github.com/conan1024hao/SAEgis) ⭐2

### Post-training (RL, distillation, calibration)

#### 🟢 [2605.06139] LPO — Listwise Policy Optimization for RLVR
- **Problem:** Group-based RLVR (GRPO et al.) optimizes an implicit target distribution without explicit projection.
- **Method:** Reframes group-policy gradient as target-projection on the response simplex; uses exact divergence minimization with bounded zero-sum self-correcting gradients.
- **Key result:** Consistently improves over policy-gradient baselines under matched targets; preserves stability and response diversity (no headline number).
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.06139) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.06139)

#### 🟢 [2605.00425] AEM — Adaptive Entropy Modulation for Multi-Turn Agentic RL
- **Problem:** Sparse outcome rewards give weak credit assignment in long agentic trajectories.
- **Method:** Response-level entropy proxy that rescales advantages, naturally transitioning exploration→exploitation without dense supervision.
- **Key result:** Consistent gains on ALFWorld / WebShop / SWE-bench-Verified (1.5B–32B); +1.4% on top of SOTA SWE-bench RL.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.00425) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.00425)

#### 🟢 [2605.08063] Flow-OPD — On-Policy Distillation for Flow Matching
- **Problem:** Multi-task FM alignment suffers reward sparsity, gradient interference, and reward hacking.
- **Method:** Cultivates per-reward GRPO experts, then OPD-distills into single student with task-routing labeling and Manifold Anchor Regularization.
- **Key result:** GenEval 63→92, OCR 59→94 on SD 3.5 Medium; ~10 pt over vanilla GRPO; "teacher-surpassing" effect.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.08063) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.08063) · [GitHub](https://github.com/CostaliyA/Flow-OPD) ⭐11

#### 🟢 [2605.06597] UniSD — Unified Self-Distillation Framework
- **Problem:** SD design choices for autoregressive LLMs are studied in isolation; interactions unclear.
- **Method:** Combines multi-teacher agreement, EMA teacher, token-contrastive learning, feature matching, divergence clipping in a single sweepable framework.
- **Key result:** UniSDfull pipeline: +5.4 over base, +2.8 over strongest baseline across 6 benchmarks / 6 models.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.06597) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.06597)

#### 🟡 [2605.06702] CASCADE — Case-Based Continual Adaptation at Deployment
- **Problem:** LLM agents stop learning after deployment; experience is wasted.
- **Method:** Episodic memory with contextual-bandit case selection; no parameter updates; no-regret guarantees over long horizons.
- **Key result:** +20.9% macro success vs zero-shot across 16 tasks (medical, legal, code, web, tool, embodied).
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.06702) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.06702) · [GitHub](https://github.com/guosyjlu/CASCADE) ⭐1

#### 🟡 [2602.03473] CaRE — Bi-Level Routing MoE for 300+ Continual Tasks
- **Problem:** Class-incremental learning collapses on very long task sequences (100s of tasks).
- **Method:** Bi-level routing MoE (router-of-routers, then experts); pretrained-model backbone; OmniBenchmark-1K introduced.
- **Key result:** First continual learner to scale 100→300+ non-overlapping tasks, "outperforming all baselines by a large margin."
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.03473) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2602.03473) · [GitHub](https://github.com/LMMMEng/CaRE) ⭐5

### Inference optimization & compiler

#### 🟢 [2605.07363] MISA — Mixture of Indexer Sparse Attention
- **Problem:** DeepSeek Sparse Attention's multi-head indexer is the dominant long-context cost.
- **Method:** Treat indexer heads as MoE pool; lightweight block-statistic router activates only a few heads per query; hierarchical re-rank optional.
- **Key result:** Matches DSA on LongBench with 8/4× fewer heads on V3.2/GLM-5; 3.82× kernel speedup on H200; >92% token-selection recall.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.07363) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.07363)

#### 🟢 [2605.06105] SPEED — Shallow Prefill, Deep Decoding
- **Problem:** Long-context inference KV-cache cost grows with both prompt length and depth.
- **Method:** Layer-asymmetric KV-visibility: materialize prompt KV only in lower layers, keep decode tokens full-depth; BoS anchor preserves coherence.
- **Key result:** 51.2 vs 51.4 baseline on OLMES (Llama-3.1-8B); TTFT −33%, TPOT −22%, active KV −25% at 128K.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.06105) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.06105)

#### 🟢 [2605.06221] UniPrefill — Universal Long-Context Prefill Acceleration
- **Problem:** Existing prefill accelerators break on hybrid architectures and fight continuous batching.
- **Method:** Block-wise dynamic sparsification at the token level; vLLM-native operator with prefill-decode co-processing and tensor parallel.
- **Key result:** Up to 2.1× TTFT speedup; effect grows with concurrent requests.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.06221) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.06221) · [GitHub](https://github.com/qhfan/UniPrefill) ⭐1

#### 🟢 [2605.07243] SpecBlock — Block-Iterative Speculative Decoding
- **Problem:** Autoregressive drafters are slow; parallel drafters lose path dependence.
- **Method:** Block-iterative drafter with intra-block layer-shift and inter-block hidden-state inheritance + co-trained rank head for tree budget allocation.
- **Key result:** +8–13% mean speedup over EAGLE-3 at 44–52% drafting cost; +11–19% with cost-aware bandit adaptation.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.07243) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.07243)

#### 🟡 [2605.08044] Fast Byte Latent Transformer (BLT-D / S / DV)
- **Problem:** Byte-level LMs match token-level but generation is byte-by-byte slow.
- **Method:** BLT-D adds block-diffusion auxiliary loss for parallel byte generation; BLT-S adds local-decoder self-speculation; BLT-DV adds verification.
- **Key result:** >50% reduction in estimated memory-bandwidth cost vs BLT on generation.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.08044) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.08044)

### Retrieval / RAG / memory

#### 🟢 [2605.04615] CoREB — Multitask Code Search Benchmark + Reranker
- **Problem:** Code search is benchmarked as 1st-stage retrieval, ignoring rerankers and developer-style queries; contamination is rampant.
- **Method:** Counterfactually-rewritten LiveCodeBench problems, 5 languages, graded relevance, timed releases; fine-tunes CoREB-Reranker.
- **Key result:** Code-specialised embeddings ~2× general on code-to-code; short keyword queries collapse all models to ≈0 nDCG@10.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.04615) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.04615) · [GitHub](https://github.com/hq-bench/coreb)

### Code / web agents

#### 🟡 [2605.07177] HyperEyes — Parallel Multimodal Search Agents
- **Problem:** Multimodal agents call tools serially, accumulating redundant rounds on independent sub-retrievals.
- **Method:** Single atomic action fuses grounding+retrieval; dual-grained RL with trajectory-level cost-efficiency reward + token-level on-policy distillation.
- **Key result:** +9.9% accuracy with 5.3× fewer tool-call rounds vs strongest open-source agent (HyperEyes-30B, 6 benchmarks).
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.07177) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.07177) · [GitHub](https://github.com/DeepExperience/HyperEyes) ⭐6

#### 🟡 [2605.07510] InterLV-Search — Interleaved Multimodal Agentic Search Benchmark
- **Problem:** Existing benchmarks treat visual evidence as input or endpoint, not interleaved trajectory.
- **Method:** 2,061-example benchmark across 3 levels (active seeking → controlled offline → open-web), with multi-branch comparison samples.
- **Key result:** Best agent <50% overall accuracy; visual seeking and evidence integration are the hard parts.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.07510) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.07510) · [GitHub](https://github.com/hbhalpha/InterLV-Search-Bench) ⭐1

### Other (specialized but kept)

#### 🟡 [2605.07755] Error Control Dynamics in Recurrent State Tracking
- **Problem:** Theory of recurrent state tracking focuses on expressivity, ignoring error accumulation.
- **Method:** Proves affine recurrents (SSMs, linear attention) cannot correct errors along state-separating subspaces; characterizes within-class spread vs between-class separation.
- **Key result:** Distinguishability-ratio crossing predicts the horizon where downstream accuracy collapses.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.07755) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.07755)

#### ⚪ [2605.05558] Compute-Anchored Wages
- **Problem:** Common claim that agents will depress cognitive-labor wages via elastic supply is wrong in mechanism.
- **Method:** Agents = production technology converting compute capital → labor units; derives CAW upper bound w ≤ λ·k·r_c.
- **Key result:** Theoretical position paper — wage ceiling for cognitive labor migrates from labor market to compute-capital rental rate.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.05558) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.05558)
