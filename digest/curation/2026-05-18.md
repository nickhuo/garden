## Part 2 — Research Radar (HuggingFace Papers, English)

### TL;DR — Today's Research Wind

Today's 34-paper batch is dominated by **post-training optimization on the exploration / failure axis** — three independent papers (NudgeRL, CIPO, Look-Before-You-Leap) all attack RLVR's narrow-exploration failure mode from different angles, suggesting this is the active bottleneck for reasoning agents in late 2026. Secondary theme: **multimodal-agent skills become first-class artifacts** (MMSkills) at the same week tech-leads-club/agent-skills is climbing GitHub — a cross-source signal worth holding. Third: practical **inference-time compression** (GQLA, HodgeCover) keeps shipping incremental wins as MLA-style architectures saturate.

### Agent frameworks & self-evolution

#### 🟢 [2605.13527] MMSkills: Multimodal Skills for General Visual Agents
- **Problem:** Current "skills" are text/code only; visual agents need multimodal procedural knowledge with state-conditioned execution.
- **Method:** Agentic trajectory-to-skill Generator + branch-loaded multimodal skill agent that consults visual evidence at runtime.
- **Key result:** No quantitative result reported in abstract; paper claims framework for representing/generating/using multimodal skill packages.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.13527) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.13527) · [GitHub](https://github.com/DeepExperience/MMSkills) ⭐97

#### 🟢 [2605.15301] Solvita: Agentic Evolution for Competitive Programming
- **Problem:** Multi-agent LLM frameworks are stateless — they discard problem-solving experience between tasks.
- **Method:** Closed-loop Planner/Solver/Oracle/Hacker agents, each paired with a trainable graph-structured knowledge network; no LLM weight updates.
- **Key result:** No quantitative result reported in abstract.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.15301) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.15301)

#### 🟡 [2605.15871] AIRA-Compose / AIRA-Design: Agentic Discovery of Neural Architectures
- **Problem:** Recursive self-improvement requires LLM agents to design architectures beyond Transformers.
- **Method:** 11 agents under 24h budget search primitives at million-param scale, extrapolate to 350M/1B/3B (Transformer + Transformer-Mamba hybrids).
- **Key result:** AIRAformer-D and AIRAhybrid-D beat Llama 3.2 by 2.4% and 3.8% on downstream tasks at 1B; AIRAformer-C scales 54% better.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.15871) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.15871)

### Agent reliability, evaluation & safety

#### 🟢 [2605.16143] Look Before You Leap: Autonomous Exploration for LLM Agents
- **Problem:** Standard task-RL agents over-exploit prior knowledge → narrow, repetitive behavior in unfamiliar environments.
- **Method:** Introduce Exploration Checkpoint Coverage metric; interleave task-execution and exploration rollouts with separate verifiable rewards.
- **Key result:** No quantitative result reported in abstract; paper claims broader state/affordance discovery.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.16143) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.16143)

#### 🟢 [2605.12882] CiteVQA: Evidence Attribution for Document Intelligence
- **Problem:** Doc-VQA evaluations only score the answer — MLLMs can be right for the wrong reason (wrong citation).
- **Method:** 1,897-question benchmark across 711 PDFs (avg 40.6 pages, 7 domains, 2 languages); requires element-level bounding-box citations; Strict Attributed Accuracy metric.
- **Key result:** No headline metric in abstract; introduces "Attribution Hallucination" as failure mode + masking ablation + expert review.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.12882) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.12882) · [GitHub](https://github.com/opendatalab/CiteVQA) ⭐53

### Post-training (RL, distillation, merging, calibration)

#### 🟢 [2605.15726] NudgeRL: Strategy-Guided Exploration for RLVR
- **Problem:** RLVR can only improve trajectories it already samples; brute-force rollout scaling is expensive.
- **Method:** Strategy Nudging conditions rollouts on lightweight strategy-level contexts; reward decomposition + distillation objective without oracle supervision.
- **Key result:** No quantitative result reported in abstract; framed as structured & diversity-driven exploration.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.15726) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.15726) · [GitHub](https://github.com/tally0818/NudgeRL) ⭐4

#### 🟢 [2605.14539] CIPO: Correction-Oriented Policy Optimization with Verifiable Rewards
- **Problem:** RLVR's sparse binary rewards waste useful information in failed trajectories.
- **Method:** Convert on-policy failed trajectories into correction-oriented supervision; jointly optimize with standard RLVR — no external signals.
- **Key result:** Improves across 11 benchmarks (math reasoning + code generation, pass@K) — specific numbers not in abstract.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.14539) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.14539)

#### 🟢 [2605.11739] EffOPD: On-Policy Distillation Foresight
- **Problem:** OPD is efficient but the parameter-level mechanism is poorly understood.
- **Method:** Identifies "foresight" — early-training updates concentrate on critical modules and align with final low-rank subspace; proposes EffOPD as extrapolation step.
- **Key result:** No headline metric in abstract; claim is plug-and-play efficiency boost over standard OPD.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.11739) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.11739) · [GitHub](https://github.com/caiyuchen-ustc/EffOPD) ⭐12

#### 🟢 [2605.02290] CoRD: Collaborative Multi-Teacher Long-CoT Distillation
- **Problem:** Existing Long-CoT distillation selects whole traces post-hoc, missing collaboration between heterogeneous teachers.
- **Method:** Step-wise multi-teacher decoding guided by predictive perplexity scoring + beam search; teachers jointly construct trajectories.
- **Key result:** "Near teacher-level student performance with fewer, structured supervision signals" — specific delta not in abstract.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.02290) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.02290) · [GitHub](https://github.com/DISL-Lab/CoRD) ⭐1

### Inference optimization & compiler

#### 🟢 [2605.15250] GQLA: Group-Query Latent Attention for Hardware-Adaptive Decoding
- **Problem:** MLA (DeepSeek-V2/V3) trained weights expose only an MQA-absorb path → tied to H100, no MTP gain on H20 / commodity GPUs.
- **Method:** GQLA exposes two algebraically equivalent decoding paths over the same weights (MQA-absorb + per-group GQA); runtime picks per hardware.
- **Key result:** Single weight set "pins the roofline" on both H100 and H20 — no retraining, no custom kernels. Specific throughput numbers not in abstract.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.15250) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.15250)

#### 🟡 [2605.13997] HodgeCover: Higher-Order Topological Compression of Sparse MoE
- **Problem:** Pairwise-only MoE merge scores miss triplet incompatibilities — three experts can be pairwise compatible but irreducibly cyclic together.
- **Method:** Hodge decomposition on a 2-complex (vertices=experts, edges=pairwise KL, faces=triplet barriers); HodgeCover greedily covers harmonic-critical triangles.
- **Key result:** No headline metric in abstract; framed as learning-free MoE compression.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.13997) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.13997)

### Multimodal, world models, generation

#### 🟢 [2605.15298] PhysBrain 1.0 VLA Technical Report
- **Problem:** Robot trajectories alone give VLA models limited physical-commonsense coverage.
- **Method:** Convert large-scale human egocentric video into structured physical-commonsense QA; capability-preserving language-sensitive adaptation to VLA policy.
- **Key result:** SOTA across ERQA, PhysBench, SimplerEnv-WidowX, LIBERO, RoboCasa with especially strong out-of-domain on SimplerEnv. No specific deltas in abstract.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.15298) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.15298) · [GitHub](https://github.com/Phys-Brain/PhysBrain-VLA) ⭐4

#### 🟡 [2605.15256] ReactiveGWM: Steering NPCs in Reactive Game World Models
- **Problem:** Current game world models treat NPCs as background pixels, can't model player↔NPC interaction.
- **Method:** Decouple player controls (lightweight additive bias on diffusion backbone) from NPC behaviors (cross-attention with Offense/Control/Defense labels); game-agnostic.
- **Key result:** Zero-shot strategy transfer claimed; no quantitative result reported in abstract.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.15256) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.15256) · [GitHub](https://github.com/INV-WZQ/ReactiveGWM) ⭐32

### Other (kept, narrow but useful)

#### ⚪ [2604.09839] Steered LLM Activations are Non-Surjective
- **Problem:** Is activation-steered behavior achievable via prompts? (Interpretability + safety question.)
- **Method:** Cast as surjectivity problem on the residual stream; prove preimages don't exist under practical assumptions; empirical check on 3 LLMs.
- **Key result:** Steering "almost surely" pushes residual stream off prompt-reachable manifold — no prompt can reproduce it. Conceptual proof; no benchmark.
- *Links:* [PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.09839) · [HF](https://huggingface.co/papers/2604.09839) · [GitHub](https://github.com/aamixsh/invertsteer)
