# Collison Installation

## Summary

A named user-acquisition technique from [[Paul Graham]]'s essay, after Stripe's Collison brothers. When a prospective user agreed to try the product, the founders didn't accept the soft "I'll sign up later" — they would say **"Right then, give me your laptop"** and set it up on the spot, in person, immediately.

## The principle

It collapses the gap between *intent* and *activation*. Most "yeses" die in the friction of later setup; the founder removes that friction by doing the install themselves, right now. It is a maximally unscalable but maximally effective form of onboarding — a concrete instance of [[Do Things That Don't Scale]].
