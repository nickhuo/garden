# Do Things That Don't Scale

## Summary

The umbrella principle from [[Paul Graham]]'s 2013 essay: **startups don't take off by themselves — the founders make them take off**, and the way they do it early is by deliberately doing labor-intensive things that *won't* work at scale. These unscalable acts generate the initial momentum that scalable growth later compounds, and they teach the founders what to actually build.

The deep error it corrects is the **"better mousetrap" fallacy** — the belief that a sufficiently good product draws users on its own ("a broad, hard-beaten road to his house"). It doesn't. Demand has to be manufactured by hand at first.

## The catalog of unscalable moves

- **Recruit** users one at a time, in person. You can't delegate early sales.
- **Delight** them with service so attentive it seems pathological — *"the experience of being your user"* is what must be insanely great, not (yet) the product.
- **Fire** — start in a [[Deliberately Narrow Initial Market]] you can dominate.
- **Meraki / by hand** — assemble hardware or run operations manually before automating (→ [[Manual-First MVP]]).
- **Consult** — treat one early user as the entire market.
- **No big launch** — momentum comes from satisfying the first small user base, not broadcast.

## Why it works

Early startups are fragile and easy to misjudge "by mature-company standards." Doing unscalable things (a) creates the demand that doesn't yet exist, (b) yields the **best feedback the founders will ever get** through direct contact, and (c) seeds durable company character — the unscalable habit (obsessive acquisition, manufacturing insight, customer obsession) often persists as the company scales.

## Connections

- Formalized by the [[Startup Idea as Vector]] model — the unscalable work *is* the second component of the idea.
- [[Collison Installation]] and [[Manual-First MVP]] are specific named techniques under this umbrella.
- Soft bridge to [[Verifiability]]'s "founder wedge" (Karpathy): pick a valuable/verifiable/undertrained domain, then *also* do the unscalable bootstrap to get the first users.
