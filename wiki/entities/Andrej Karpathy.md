# Andrej Karpathy

## Summary

AI researcher and educator known for articulating how the software stack itself is changing under machine learning. His **Software 2.0** essay (2017) reframed neural-net weights as a new kind of code — programs compiled from data rather than written by hand. In his 2025 "Software Is Changing (Again)" talk he extended this to **Software 3.0** (programming in natural-language prompts) and argued LLMs behave like a new operating system: a few providers host complex evolving platforms on which other software is built. He also reframed the agent hype as "the decade of agents," favoring partial autonomy with humans in the loop over full autonomy.

## Why he matters here

Karpathy is the clearest articulator of the historical arc behind [[Model-Centric Architecture]]: logic steadily migrating out of hand-written code and into the model. His "keep a human-controllable autonomy slider" stance also supplies the *user keeps final control* leg of the model-centric thesis.
