# Geoffrey Huntley

Software engineer and prolific blogger (ghuntley.com) on agentic coding. Best known in this wiki for coining the **[[Ralph Loop]]** — a monolithic, single-process, one-task-per-loop coding-agent pattern — and for the strongly-stated thesis that **agentic coding is [[Context Engineering]], not multi-agent orchestration**.

## Positions

- LLMs are "a new form of programmable computer" — program them, don't just use them to type faster.
- Traditional brick-by-brick software engineering ("Jenga stacks") is obsolete; loops produce software cheaper and faster. "Software development is dead – I killed it."
- Against premature [[Multi-Agent Systems]]: agents are non-deterministic, so multiplexing them multiplies complexity. Prefers vertical scaling of one process.
- Hiring stance: won't hire engineers who can't demonstrate a coding agent they built themselves.

## Work

- **The Weaving Loom** — his "infrastructure for evolutionary software" project (self-described automation "level 9," past Steve Yegge's "Gas Town" at level 8). Endpoint of the [[Ralph Loop]] philosophy: revenue-optimizing autonomous loops with no human in the loop. Source not public.
- Free workshop / YouTube series: "how to build a coding agent."
- Other posts referenced: "llm weights vs the papercuts of corporate", "don't waste your back pressure", "cognitive security", "AI as economic warfare".
