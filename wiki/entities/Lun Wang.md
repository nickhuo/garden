# Lun Wang

ML researcher; background in **computer security & privacy** (UC Berkeley), with published work on Byzantine-robust federated learning. Formerly at **Google DeepMind** (and Google). Web: `wanglun1996.github.io`, GitHub `wanglun1996`.

On **2026-05-17**, the day he left Google DeepMind, he published [[2026-05-17 - Lun Wang - Your Evals Will Break]] — a thesis-on-departure arguing the AI field has the bottleneck wrong: it is not compute, data, energy, or architecture, but **evaluation**. The essay applies a statistical-mechanics framing (order parameters, phase transitions) to LLM capability shifts and argues current evals are *structurally reactive*.
