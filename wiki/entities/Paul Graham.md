# Paul Graham

Programmer-turned-essayist-and-investor. Co-founded **Viaweb** (an early web-app store builder, acquired by Yahoo and rebranded Yahoo Store) and, in 2005, **[[Y Combinator]]** — the accelerator that institutionalized much of modern early-stage startup practice. His essays at paulgraham.com are foundational reading for founders.

## In this wiki

- **[[2013-07 - Paul Graham - Do Things that Don't Scale]]** — his canonical essay on early-stage growth; the source for [[Do Things That Don't Scale]], [[Startup Idea as Vector]], [[Collison Installation]], [[Manual-First MVP]], and [[Deliberately Narrow Initial Market]]. Viaweb appears in it as a worked example of [[Manual-First MVP]] (building stores for merchants by hand).
