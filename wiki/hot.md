2026-05-31 — ingested **Opsahl-Ong et al., "Optimizing Instructions and Demonstrations for Multi-Stage Language Model Programs"** (EMNLP 2024; arXiv 2406.11695; [[2024-06 - Opsahl-Ong et al - MIPRO Optimizing Instructions and Demonstrations]]) — seed gate **14/14 → high**. This is the primary source for **[[MIPRO|MIPROv2]]**, the prompt optimizer the wiki has been *citing* across [[Prompt Optimization]], [[GEPA]], and [[DSPy]] without ever sourcing — so this is a **lineage backfill**, not a new direction. Problem: optimize the prompts of a multi-module [[Compound AI System|LM program]] against an end-to-end metric with **no module-level labels and no gradients**. Three moves answer three challenges: (1) **bootstrap demonstrations** — keep per-module I/O from successful program traces as self-generated few-shot demos; (2) **grounded instruction proposal** — a proposer LM writes instructions conditioned on program/data/demos/history/tips (grounding is the contribution); (3) **TPE Bayesian surrogate** over (instruction, demo-set) per module on stochastic mini-batches, with *implicit* credit assignment. Task model Llama-3-8B, proposer GPT-3.5/GPT-4; 7 programs (HotPotQA, HotPotQA-Conditional, Iris, Iris-Typo, Heart Disease, ScoNe, HoVer). **Wins 5/7 by up to ~13%** (HotPotQA 36.1→46.4; HoVer 25.3→39.0). **Three lessons:** demonstrations are the highest-impact lever; optimizing both wins overall; instructions matter most for conditional-rule tasks. **Key tension logged as `[!contradiction]` on both pages:** MIPRO (2024) says *demos dominate, optimize both*; [[GEPA]] (2026) reverses to *instruction-only wins* on newer LLMs — same [[DSPy]] family, different model era. Closes the optimizer lineage **few-shot → MIPRO → GEPA**. New: concept [[MIPRO]], entity [[Krista Opsahl-Ong]] (MIPRO lead + GEPA co-author). **Citation chase: none built** — MIPRO is the primary source; upstreams (DSPy already filed, OPRO, TPE/Bergstra, Wan et al.) documented in a Lineage section.

2026-05-31 — ingested **Chayenne Zhao, "SGLang Omni: Redesigning the Inference Framework for Multi-Stage Generative Models"** (X long-form article, login-gated — full text supplied by Nick; [[2026-05-30 - Chayenne Zhao - SGLang Omni Multi-Stage Inference]]) — seed gate **12/14 → high**. The wiki's **first inference-serving-systems source** (author is a core [[SGLang]] contributor who moved from SGLang RL to SGLang Omni). Methodology thesis: **slice models by computation, not modality** — the right inference axis is whether *decoding* is single-stage or **[[Multi-Stage Decoding]]** (heterogeneous stages that alternate). Multi-stage models ([[Qwen3-Omni]], TTS like Fish S2 Pro, omni-output like Ming Omni) share **three computational characteristics → three mechanisms** (the article's spine): heterogeneous compute paradigms → **scheduling decoupling** (one `Scheduler`/stage); divergent dependencies → **layered communication** (ZMQ control plane + relay data plane, CUDA IPC/NCCL/RDMA); cross-stage memory contention → **cross-stage memory budgeting**. All three captured in [[Stage-Decoupled Inference Architecture]]. Canonical pipeline [[Thinker-Talker-MTP]] ([[Qwen3-Omni]]): Thinker (AR text, compute-then-memory-bound) → Talker (AR 0-th codec token, **latency-bound**) ↔ MTP (parallel codec completion + write-back). **Key insight: a third roofline category** beyond compute-/memory-bound — latency-bound decode where *kernel-launch + sync overhead* dominate; fixed by fusing the tight Talker↔MTP loop into one Stage / **piecewise CUDA Graph** (not more FLOPs/bandwidth). Encoders are first-class memory citizens (~2.5 GB weights but **>30 GB activation** for a 1-min video). New entities [[SGLang]], [[Chayenne Zhao]], [[Qwen3-Omni]]; 3 concepts [[Multi-Stage Decoding]], [[Stage-Decoupled Inference Architecture]], [[Thinker-Talker-MTP]]. **Strong bridge:** [[Interaction Model Architecture]] (TML) is the two-stage case — same latency-bound/heavier-work split, also contributed streaming sessions to [[SGLang]]; cross-ref + callout added there. Also answers the [[LLM]] domain's standing "SGLang adoption" question. **Citation chase: none built** — primary source; only external ref is the author's unlinked "earlier blog post" on Qwen3-Omni decoding.

2026-05-30 — ingested **GEPA: Reflective Prompt Evolution Can Outperform Reinforcement Learning** (Agrawal et al., ICLR 2026 **Oral**; [[2026-02 - Agrawal et al - GEPA Reflective Prompt Evolution]]) — seed gate **14/14 → high** (UC Berkeley/Stanford/MIT/Databricks; last author **[[Omar Khattab]]**, creator of [[DSPy]]). Thesis: **language is a richer learning medium than scalar reward** — [[GEPA]] (Genetic-Pareto) reflects on natural-language execution + *evaluation* traces (compiler errors, failed rubrics before they collapse to a number) to mutate prompts, beating **[[GRPO]]** (24k rollouts) by up to **20% at 35× fewer rollouts** and **MIPROv2** by **>10%**. Three pillars: reflective prompt mutation (a **feedback function `µ_f`** + implicit credit assignment), genetic evolution with a Merge crossover, and [[Pareto-based Candidate Selection]] (per-instance Pareto frontier sampling — the **dominant** driver: +12.44% vs +6.05% greedy / +5.11% beam). Also: instruction-only > joint instruction+few-shot, prompts **9.2× shorter**, **+9% cross-model transfer** (Qwen3-8B→GPT-4.1-Mini), and extensions as inference-time search (NPU/CUDA kernels) + adversarial prompt search (AIME pass@1 76%→10%). New entities [[Omar Khattab]] + [[DSPy]]; 6 concepts [[GEPA]], [[Language Feedback as Learning Signal]], [[Pareto-based Candidate Selection]], [[Compound AI System]], [[Prompt Optimization]], [[GRPO]]. **Key bridge: [[GEPA]] is the prompt-space twin of [[Heuristic Learning]]** (Weng) — both learn by editing an interpretable, LLM-editable artifact (prompts vs code) from trace/test feedback instead of gradients; cross-refs added to [[Heuristic Learning]] and a friendly amendment to [[Verifiability]] (RLVR collapses the verifiable signal to a scalar and throws away the language). **Citation chase: none built** — GEPA *is* the primary source; its ~100 refs (GRPO/Shao, MIPROv2/Opsahl-Ong, DSPy, TextGrad, Reflexion) are documented in a Lineage section rather than fetched.

## Key Recent Facts

- **The thesis in one loop.** Real-time learning, persistent memory, model-centric architecture, and online evaluation are four faces of one system: interaction → **online eval** scores it → **real-time learning** turns the score into an update (durability spectrum) → **persistent memory/persona** stores it → the **model** (not orchestration code) acts on it, user keeps final control. See [[Research - Continually-Learning Model-Centric Systems]].
- **Real-time learning is a durability spectrum** ([[Online Learning from Interaction]]): in-context ([[In-Context Learning]]) → memory write-back ([[Self-Editing Memory]]) → test-time training ([[Test-Time Adaptation]]) → continual online RL. Cheapest/reversible → most durable/dangerous. Catastrophic forgetting is the central unsolved problem; memory is the main mitigation. [[Welcome to the Era of Experience]] (Silver & Sutton 2025) is the "why now": grounded environmental reward over human-prejudgment reward.
- **Persistence has two poles** ([[Persona Vectors vs Memory Files]]): **persona vectors** (parametric, causal activation-space directions, direct control over *who* the agent is, low inspectability — [[Persona Vectors]], [[Activation Steering / Representation Engineering]]) vs **memory files** (contextual, inspectable record of *what* it knows — [[Memory Stream]], [[Self-Editing Memory]], [[MemGPT]]/[[Letta]]). Complementary, not competing.
- **Model-centric vs harness/schema is a slider** ([[Model-Centric Architecture]]). Scaling favors the model long-run ([[The Bitter Lesson]], [[Software 2.0]]); ship-time reliability favors structure the model can't provide (Donut +22% workflow success, −85% invalid calls). The durable side-code is the guarantees layer (constraints, validation, permissioning) — [[Code-to-the-Side vs Orchestration]].
- **Online evaluation is the bridge** ([[Online Evaluation]]): implicit/explicit signal → [[LLM-as-Judge]] or [[Reward Modeling]] → update to retrieval/rubric/policy/persona. Spectrum from cheap-observational ([[Implicit Feedback Signals]]) to slow-causal ([[A/B Testing for Agents]]); rests on [[Eval Validity]]. A strong LLM judge matches human agreement (>80%) only after debiasing.
- **Connection to Nick's work is the organizing principle.** Online evaluation + schema/contract discipline is Nick's center of gravity (Compass dual-judge/RAGAS, Donut golden eval, Beckman two-axis judge). Real-time learning extends Sonic (capture) + Donut (flywheel); persona vectors extend Beckman's mastery overlay + (A,B,C) metacognition fit.
